What's up with the title you might be asking yourself. Well the title is because when I started this, I am just a curious QA guy in software and have been working in the software industry for over 12 years with no real know how in coding. So I wanted to take some classes and found out that maybe having access to some tools and buying some videos was the way to go. Just reading wasn't cutting it anymore.

So, I bought a video course pack from StackSkills and the first one I opened up was this full stack series from Devslopes, and those guys are brogrammers to be sure. But, I like being treated like a friend instead of a student, so these guys are pretty good when it comes to that, and they had apparently only freshly integrated the idea to keep their mistakes in videos, and show them correcting their mistakes, so that's pretty neat philosophy too.

Next I plan on doing a few videos on python or tutorials on using test cafe. But that's where we're at right now.
